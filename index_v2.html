MSc Project

This webpage is part of the deliverables for the 2024/2025 MSc Project at the College of Science & Engineering, University of Glasgow.

Research Question

Research Context

Urban green spaces (UGS) play a crucial role in enhancing quality of life, promoting public health, and mitigating urban heat island effects. This research project investigates the spatial distribution and accessibility of green spaces in major European cities using advanced geospatial analysis techniques.

The study focuses on developing a standardized methodology for assessing green space equity, incorporating factors such as population density, transportation networks, and socioeconomic indicators.

Research Methodology

Data Collection

Utilized open data sources including OpenStreetMap, Copernicus Land Monitoring Service, and municipal GIS portals. Collected datasets include:

  • Land use/land cover classifications
  • Public transportation networks
  • Population distribution data
  • Green space boundaries and attributes

Technical Approach (Software & Platform)

Implemented a multi-criteria analysis framework using:

  • QGIS for spatial data processing
  • PostgreSQL/PostGIS for data management
  • Python (GeoPandas, NetworkX) for network analysis
  • Mapbox GL JS for interactive visualization

Key Analysis Steps

1

Service Area Delineation

Created 10-minute walking isochrones from all green space entrances using pedestrian network analysis. Adjusted for real-world barriers like rivers and highways.

2

Accessibility Scoring

Developed a composite accessibility index incorporating distance, route quality, and crossing difficulty. Validated through field surveys in sample neighborhoods.

3

Spatial Statistics

Applied Getis-Ord Gi* hotspot analysis to identify statistically significant clusters of underserved populations relative to green space distribution.
